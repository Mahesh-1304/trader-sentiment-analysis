📊 Trader Behavior vs Market Sentiment — Summary
Methodology

The analysis combines Bitcoin market sentiment data with Hyperliquid trading data by aligning both datasets at a daily level. Key performance metrics such as PnL, win rate, and a drawdown proxy (PnL standard deviation) were computed. Behavioral indicators including trade frequency, position size, and directional bias were analyzed. Traders were also segmented into frequent vs infrequent and high-risk vs low-risk groups.

Key Insights
Performance is Similar Across Sentiments
Average PnL and win rates remain nearly identical between Fear and Greed periods, indicating sentiment does not directly impact profitability.
Risk is Higher During Fear
PnL variability is significantly higher during Fear periods, suggesting increased uncertainty and risk exposure.
Behavior Changes with Sentiment
Traders increase position sizes and show stronger long bias during Greed periods, indicating overconfidence-driven behavior.

📊 Actionable Strategies
1. Risk Reduction in Fear Periods

During Fear market conditions, traders should reduce position sizes and avoid aggressive trades. The analysis shows that while average returns remain similar, volatility increases significantly (~14%), leading to higher risk without additional reward. A disciplined, low-risk approach is more effective in such environments.

2. Avoid Overexposure in Greed Periods

During Greed periods, traders tend to increase position sizes due to overconfidence. However, this does not result in significantly higher returns or win rates. Traders should maintain consistent position sizing and avoid excessive risk-taking to ensure stable performance.

3. Favor Consistent Trading Behavior

Frequent traders demonstrate more stable performance compared to infrequent traders. Adopting a strategy based on smaller, repeated trades rather than large, sporadic bets can improve consistency and reduce performance volatility
